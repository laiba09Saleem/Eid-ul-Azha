About Eid ul Azha

The Significance

Eid ul Azha commemorates the willingness of Prophet Ibrahim (AS) to sacrifice his son Ismail (AS) as an act of obedience to Allah's command. At the last moment, Allah replaced Ismail with a ram, which was to be slaughtered instead.

This event is mentioned in the Quran and teaches us the importance of submission to Allah's will.

Traditions

On this day, Muslims around the world perform the following:

  • Special Eid prayers in congregation
  • Sacrifice of an animal (Qurbani)
  • Distribution of meat to family, friends, and the needy
  • Wearing new clothes and visiting relatives
  • Exchanging gifts and greetings

Eid ul Azha Duas

Dua for Eid Day

اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْأَلُكَ خَيْرَ مَا فِي هَذَا الْيَوْمِ، وَخَيْرَ مَا بَعْدَهُ، وَأَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ شَرِّ مَا فِي هَذَا الْيَوْمِ، وَشَرِّ مَا بَعْدَهُ

"O Allah, I ask You for the goodness of this day and the goodness of what follows it, and I seek refuge in You from the evil of this day and the evil of what follows it."

Dua for Acceptance of Sacrifice

إِنِّي وَجَّهْتُ وَجْهِيَ لِلَّذِي فَطَرَ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ حَنِيفًا وَمَا أَنَا مِنَ الْمُشْرِكِينَ

"Indeed, I have turned my face toward He who created the heavens and the earth, inclining toward truth, and I am not of those who associate others with Allah."

General Dua for Eid

تَقَبَّلَ اللَّهُ مِنَّا وَمِنْكُمْ

"May Allah accept (good deeds) from us and from you."
